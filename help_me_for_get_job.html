What this is

This tool reads patent search exports (Engineering Village CSV format) and turns them into a job-hunting database. The core idea: companies that file patents in cheminformatics / chemistry / pharma are companies actively investing in R&D in those areas — they're the ones likely to be hiring. Inventors listed on those patents are also strong potential contacts (e.g. for LinkedIn outreach).

Workflow

  1. Load CSVs on the Dashboard tab. Multiple files merge automatically.
  2. Dashboard: get a feel for which companies, countries, and tech areas dominate your dataset.
  3. Companies tab: ranked list of every assignee. Click a row to see their patents and inventors. Click the ★ to add to your outreach list.
  4. Patents tab: full-text search to find patents matching specific topics (e.g. "machine learning molecule") — the assignees of those hits are your most relevant targets.
  5. Outreach list: track who you've contacted, paste in HR emails / LinkedIn URLs as notes, set status. Export when ready.

Tips for cheminfo job search

  • Search for terms like "machine learning", "neural network", "in silico", "QSAR", "docking", "generative", "deep learning" — companies whose patents mention these are using your skills.
  • IPC sections G06N (AI/ML), G16C (cheminformatics), C07 (organic chemistry), A61K (pharma formulations) are high-signal.
  • Sort companies by most recent activity — a company that filed in the last 1–2 years is far more likely to have an open R&D budget than one that stopped 5 years ago.
  • Inventors who appear on multiple recent patents from the same company are usually team leads — those are the people to message on LinkedIn.

Privacy

Everything runs in your browser. CSVs are not uploaded anywhere. Your starred companies and notes are saved in localStorage on this device only.
